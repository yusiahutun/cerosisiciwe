My hero big brother: Heart-wrenching moment a soldier's grieving sister collapses in front of his grave at Arlington Cemetery

This is the heart-wrenching moment a sister was so overcome with grief that she lay down and sobbed on the grave of her brother, who served in both Iraq and Afghanistan.

Lesleigh Coyer, 26, traveled to Washington’s Arlington National Cemetery with her parents on Monday to pay respects to her brother, Army Ranger Staff Sgt. Ryan Coyer, on the first anniversary of his death.

Her only sibling served two tours of duty during the war in Afghanistan and died unexpectedly of cardiac arrest last year after sustaining battle injuries.

Grief: Lesleigh Coyer, 26, of Saginaw, Michigan, curled up in front of her brother Ryan's grave today; he served as an Army Ranger and unexpectedly died last year after serving in both Afghanistan and Iraq

Loss: Tony Coyer puts his hand on the shoulder of his daughter Lesleigh as his wife Mary weeps while visiting the grave of their son, who died a year ago tomorrow

Elite: Ryan Coyer was an Army Ranger and part of the prestigious tan berets

Sgt Coyer passed away unexpectedly on March 12, 2012 at the age of 26.

He had suffered a cardiac arrest following injuries he sustained in Afghanistan.

The family, from Saginaw, Michigan, returned to Arlington to mark the first-year anniversary of his death.

Ryan Coyer was born in Nashville, Tennessee but grew up in Saginaw when his parents moved to the town when he was young, according to mlive.com.

A 2004 graduate of Arthur Hill High, Ryan had played on the school football team and was a keen weightlifter while also keeping a place on the honor roll.

He enlisted in the Army that fall and served in the 1st Battalion, 75th Ranger Division which is stationed out of Hunter Arm Airfield in Georgia.

Ms Coyer is two years younger than he was, and said in a 2012 interview with the Saginaw News that he was her ‘best friend'.

‘I looked up to him,’ she said. ‘I leaned on him, just as he did me.

'He was a great man and I was honored to be his sister.’

The 75th Ranger Regiment is a prestigious branch of the Army.

According to the military website, the elite group carries out complex special missions and are recognized by their tan berets. 

His parents, Anthony and Mary Coyer, who are both in their fifties, told the Saginaw News last year that they were proud of his decision to enlist.

‘He did it on his own and he came home one day and told his mom and dad what he was going to do,’ Mr Coyer told the paper at the time.

Close bond: Lesleigh Coyer dances with her brother Ryan. She describes him as her 'brother, best friend and hero'

Ever the best of friends: Ryan and Lesleigh Coyer pictured playing together as children

Role model: Lesleigh, right, said shortly after her brother's death that she always admired him and was honored to be his sister

Following his training, Sgt Coyer served both in Iraq and Afghanistan.

Located in Arlington, Virginia, the national cemetery serves as the final resting place for hundreds of thousands of soldiers.

The cemetery holds between 27 and 30 funerals each day, and is open 365 days a year since being established at the height of the Civil War in 1864. Presidents John F Kennedy and William Howard Taft are both buried at the cemetery. 

There are hundreds of memorials across the cemetery's 600 acres. There are markers for the space shuttle Challenger and Columbia disasters; for those who died in the Iran Rescue Mission of 1980 and the September 11 attack on the Pentagon, among many others.
