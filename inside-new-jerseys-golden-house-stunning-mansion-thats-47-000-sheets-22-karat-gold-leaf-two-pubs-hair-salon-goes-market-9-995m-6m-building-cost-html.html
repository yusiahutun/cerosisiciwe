Inside New Jersey's GOLDEN house: Stunning mansion that's made out of 47,000 sheets of 22-karat gold

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, September 15, 2024

A married couple are selling their eye-popping New Jersey mansion made of real gold for $9.995 million - which is $6 million less than it cost them to build it.

The current homeowners Dan, 63, and Sheree Funsch, 57, used 47,000 sheets of 22-karat gold leaf to build the home.

Dan had promised Sheree he was going to 'build you the biggest, most beautiful home, and I’ll marry you' when they were first dating, and that's what he did. 

The couple, who both worked as executives in the beauty industry and met in 2005, finished the construction of the 16,000 square foot Château Sheree in 2012 after buying the property in 2007 for $2 million and spending $14 million on building and landscaping.

Their time, effort and money resulted in a bedazzled mansion just 15 miles northwest of New York City - complete with eight bedrooms, 13 fireplaces, a home theater and two bars.
