How to shop at auctions: The novice guide

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, March 26, 2024

Rex

For rare and unique treasures, auction houses are a go-to destination. With the season of gift-giving upon us, auctions also serve an ideal place to find one-of-a-kind gems, whether jewellery, interiors or art. But there’s something about stepping inside these storied institutions that can feel intimidating.

We spoke to Christie’s head of European works of art, Anna Evans, to dispel some of the myths surrounding auctions and also to talk through the bidding process.

You don’t have to be rich to buy at auction

“A key myth is that you need to have an abundance of wealth to buy at Christie’s, this is not the case. Christie's specifically caters for all budgets and tastes; we offer in over 80 different collecting categories from paintings and furniture, to wine, Chinese works of art, ski posters and decorative objects.”

Don’t be put off by stereotypes of auctions; everyone is welcome

“Christie’s has an open-door policy and welcomes everyone to enjoy art and appreciate culture. Our sales cover more than 80 categories, including furniture, fine art, antiquities and jewellery, from £800 and up - therefore we have something for any type of buyer. There is also no dress code at auction, please come as you are. Our monthly ‘Late’ events are free to attend and give you the opportunity to listen to talks and participate in interactive demonstrations."

Decide what you love

“Ultimately, the first step of collecting and buying is to purchase pieces that you will love to live with. Buying items that have an interesting history always provide you with a story and could help increase the value over time. Also, you don’t limit yourself, be open-minded and explore different categories.”

Use the site and pre-sale views as a catalogue

“The first step is to research your category and decide what you love, then browse what is currently for sale. Look through our schedule of upcoming auctions as well as a list of what’s currently on view in our global showrooms. At our pre-sale views you have the opportunity to speak with a specialist in person about the items you are interested in. The pre-auction viewing is free of charge and all are welcome. Christie’s typically installs works for public viewing three or four days before auction.”

Christie's

Jussi Pylkkanen, Global President at Christie’s

Create an account and choose your bidding method

“Whether in person, telephone, online or by absentee bid, work out how you want to place your bid. “If you’re a first-time buyer, I would suggest coming in person to experience the excitement at a live auction in one of our salesrooms around the world. However you decide to participate, you then decide your top price and when to stop bidding. Remember to check the buyers premium and any other additional fees on you items before you bid. Once you win your object it is just a matter of payment and collection.”

Do feel free to use the public viewings as museums

“Attend a public viewing, which takes place approximately three to four days before the sale date. Our doors are open to everyone even those who are just browsing; we are a museum where everything is for sale. Additionally, speak to a specialist, they are always on hand to answer key questions about the item, its condition, its estimated price – take advantage of their expertise as they are passionate about collecting and love to offer advice.”

Auctions are great for gift-buying

"The interiors sales offer great variety for finding the perfect present. Our 28 November Interiors sale, for example, has everything from beautiful agate boxes, novelty silver tableware and chic ski posters to Chinese ceramics and bronzes by renowned South African artist Dylan Lewis. There is something for all tastes and budgets."
