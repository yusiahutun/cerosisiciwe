Post Malone's 'greatest fear' caused him to make huge lifestyle changes

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, June 18, 2024

Post Malone is the latest celebrity to join Alex Cooper on the iconic Call Her Daddy podcast, as he opens up about his greatest fear, life after becoming a dad, and his failed marriage proposal to his now fiance.

The music star rose to fame in 2015, after the huge success of his single White Iverson. His life has changed massively professionally and personally since then, and now he’s baring all on the pod.

Post Malone reveals his greatest fear on Call Her Daddy

Opening up to Alex, who asked him what his greatest fear was, Post Malone, whose real name is Austin Post, said becoming a father has made him rethink some of his habits.

“I guess not being able to be there for my baby, which is a new fear,” he explained. “That’s why I tried to slow down on drinking, to take better care of my body.”

Austin welcomed his first daughter just over a year ago in May 2022, with his fiance whose name is unknown.

Although they are happily engaged, the engagement didn’t go down too well the first time, and he actually had to propose twice. Ouch!

The star opens up on his failed engagement

The Sunflower singer made the awkward revelation that he actually had to prose to his fiance twice after she initially said no the first time around in Vegas.

“I had lost a significant amount of money at the table, and we go upstairs and I’m, like, off my rocker hammered,” the celebrity star revealed. For some reason, he decided then would be a good time to propose, but his girlfriend was less than impressed.

 “I was like, ‘Hey, you wanna marry me? I got a ring and all this stuff, and then she said no,” he recalled. Luckily, not all hope was lost as she then told him to ask the next tomorrow, which he did, successfully this time.

Post Malone opens up on being bullied

In another part of the interview, the star opened up on how he was bullied during school. He revealed that he’d always been an “introverted kid” and was picked on for the “way he dressed”.

“I loved to express, I love making music forever, I love playing games. Weird, a little weirdo.

“I moved when I was nine, and then I used to get bullied a whole bunch in school, I guess for dressing the way I did. We wore skinny jeans and all that stuff, and that was just kind of like a new deal,” the award-winning singer explained.

I don’t know. And people would throw gum in my hair… Kids are mean,” he concluded.
