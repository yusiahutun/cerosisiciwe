Who is the highest paid MotoGP rider? The top 10 list in 2021

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, April 17, 2024

MotoGP is one of the top sporting events in the world. And like most such events, the professionals who participate earn vast amounts of money. So, who is the highest paid MotoGP rider?

Moto GP riders earn a monthly/annual salary from the company (team) they ride for. On top of that, they receive money for participating in and winning tournaments. Lastly, the riders – who are essentially professional athletes – have sponsors, which brings them lots of money in sponsor deals.

However, they also earn more in the form of sponsorship deals. In addition, the riders also take home significant amounts of cash for participating or winning various Moto GP races held throughout the season.

Who is the highest paid MotoGP rider?

MotoGP is the pinnacle of racing on two wheels. The fastest bikes and the most talented riders in the world fill the grid. Here is a list of the highest-paid Moto GP riders in 2021:

1. Marc Marquez - $15 million

Who is the highest-paid Moto GP rider in 2021? It is none other than Marc Marquez. Marquez is a Spanish Moto GP rider who is currently signed with the Respol Honda team. He is their first choice rider, which means that he earns top dollar from the company.

Marc Marquez's salary is estimated to be around $15 million – this was after signing a new contract with Honda. On top of that, he has several sponsorship deals that total approximately $2.5 million. And with Moto GP prize money per race possibly being into tens of thousands or more, Marquez annual salary is quite significant.

2. Valentino Rossi - $10 million

Valentino Rossi is a household name in motorcycle racing. He is one of the most successful Moto GP riders of all time, with nine Grand Prix world championship titles to his name. Rossi's success on the track also translates to his bank account.

Currently, he is one of the wealthiest Moto GP riders with an estimated net worth of $200 million. Valentino Rossi's salary per race is roughly around $500,000, courtesy of his current contract with Monster Energy Yamaha. So the figure translates to about $10 million per year.

However, Rossi's total earnings per year are much higher than that. He is currently considered the highest-earning Moto GP rider, thanks to his sponsorship deals, and other business ventures. In 2013, he was ranked as one of the highest-paid athletes with an annual income of $22 million.

3. Maverick Vinales – $8 million

Maverick Vinales is not only a young and upcoming Moto GP rider but also one of the most successful. The young Spanish rider is currently ranked number 6 on the Moto GP standings and already has one win to his name.

Vinales is currently contracted to the Yamaha MotoGP team, where he is a regular rider. He earns around $421,000 per race, translating to $8 million per year. The rider also makes about $1.5 million a year from sponsorship deals.

4. Joan Mir – $6 million

Joan Mir Maryata is a 23-year old Spanish Grand Prix rider who currently rides for the Suzuki Ecstar racing team. He is famous for winning the 2017 Moto3 and 2020 Moto GP world championships. He started his career in the Moto GP Rookies Cup and Moto 3 Junior World Championships.

After that, he entered the Moto3 World Championship, riding for the Leopard Racing Team. In 2017, Mir won the 2017 championship and moved to Moto2. Then, after just one season, he entered the Moto GP and has been racing for the Suzuki team.

Joan is currently on a 2-year contract deal with Suzuki that expires in 2022. He earns a base salary of $6 million a year, or roughly $316,000 per race. On top of that, he has various sponsorship deals that see him earn an additional $1 million a year.

5. Pol Espargaro - $ 3.5 million

Pol Espargaro is a Spanish Moto GP rider contracted with the Respol Honda Team. He is the younger brother to Aleix Espargaro, another professional motorcycle racer who rides for the Aprilia Racing Team.

Pol entered Moto GP in 2014, after a few years in the 125cc World Championship and Moto2 World Championship. He first signed with the Monster Yamaha Tech 3 team before switching to the Red Bull KTM Factory Racing team. In 2021, Pol signed a 2-year contract with the Respol Honda Team.

Pol Espargaro new contract with Respol Honda will see him earn about $184,500 per race. Annually, he will be taking home $3.5 million in base salary. On top of that, he has sponsorship deals estimated to be in the tune of $1 million a year.

6. Franco Morbidelli - $3 million

Franco Morbidelli is a 26-year old Italian professional motorcycle racer. He is currently contracted to the Petronas Yamaha SRT racing team. He is another up and coming rider in Moto GP who is famous for finishing second in the 2020 World Championship season.

Other notable wins include the 2013 European Superstock 600 champion and the 2017 Moto2 champion. Franco Morbidelli started training as a motorcycle racer from a young age; his father, Livio Morbidelli, was a racer himself. He trained with Valentino Rossi in a program that would later give birth to Rossi’s VR46 Academy for motorcycle racers.

Morbidelli started his racing career with the European Superstock 600 championship in 2011. After winning the 2013 season, he moved up to the Moto2 World Championship, continuing to race until the end of the 2017 season. In 2018, he moved up to the premier Moto GP class, initially representing the Marc DVDs Racing team.

In 2019, Morbidelli signed a contract with the Petronas Yamaha SRT racing team, which he extended again for two years in 2020. The motorcycle racer’s salary is estimated to be around $3 million in base pay – translating to roughly $158,000 per race. He also has sponsorship deals that are estimated to be worth approximately $500,000 per year.

7. Alex Rins – $2.5 million

Alexis Rins is a professional motorcycle racer currently competing in the Grand Prix World championship. The 25-year old Spaniard rides for the Suzuki Ecstar racing team. He is currently ranked number 14 in the current 2021 season and is notable for finishing 3rd in 2020.

Rins started his professional career in 2012 in the Moto3 championship. After two seasons, he moved to the Moto2 Championship, where he competed for two more seasons before joining the premier Moto GP class. He has been under contract with Suzuki Ecstar since joining the Grand Prix in 2017.

Alex Rins current contract, which is set to expire in 2022, sees him take home $2.5 million a year in base pay or $132,500 per race. He also has several sponsorship deals with Monster Energy, Alpinestars, NOLAN, and others.

8. Fabio Quartararo – $2.5 million

Fabio Quartararo is another youngster making waves in the Moto GP championship. At just 22 years, the Frenchman is one of the most successful motorcycle racers in the world. He is currently under contract with the Monster Energy Yamaha racing team. He has enjoyed a successful 2021 season with four podium wins already and is currently ranked number 1 with a 34 points lead.

Quartararo started racing at the young age of four years. He entered the professional circuit in 2013, beginning at the Moto3 championship before moving to Moto2 in 2017. In 2019, he joined the Grand Prix premier class, signing with the Petronas Yamaha SRT racing team.

Currently, Quartararo is on a 2-year contract that will expire in 2022. The Moto GP rider’s salary is estimated to be around $2.5 million per year. He also has several other sponsorship deals that net him an estimated $500,000 a year.

9. Johann Zarco - $2 million

Johann Zarco is another French Moto GP rider who currently represents the Ducati Pramac Racing team. The 30-year old is famous for winning the 2015 and 2016 Moto2 championship seasons. He also boasts of 16 wins across all racing classes, making him one of the most successful French riders.

Zarco started his professional career in 2009 by competing in the 125cc World Championship. In 2013, he moved to the Moto2 championship, where he competed until 2016. In the 2017 season, he joined the Moto GP, first signing with the Monster Yamaha racing team.

Currently, Zarco is under a 1-year contract with Pramac Racing. He has had a successful season and is currently ranked number 2 (behind Quartararo). His current deal sees him earn $2 million a year in base pay. He also has sponsorship deals that are estimated to be worth $500,000 a year.

10. Brad Binder - $500,000

Band Binder is a South African Moto GP racer. He is currently racing for the Red Bull KTM Factory Racing team. He is another up-and-coming rider in the Grand Prix premier class who is most famous for winning the 2016 Moto3 championship. The star started his professional motorcycle racing career in 2011.

He first competed in the 125 World Championship before moving up to Moto3, then late Moto2 and MotoGP. Binder has been racing with the KTM Factory Racing team since 2015. He is currently on a three-year contract with the company. Brad Binder's current salary is estimated to be $500,000.
