Luke Littler has grown adult darts fan 'in tears' after opening another FIFA pack on walk-on

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, September 30, 2024

Luke Littler was asked to open a FIFA pack for the second week running - leaving the grown adult darts fan seemingly in tears.

The 17-year-old was put in control of an Ultimate Team pack opening in Newcastle last week before losing in the semi-finals of night four of the Premier League to Michael van Gerwen.

And a crowd member in Exeter repeated the stunt tonight (February 29) before Littler got underway on night five against Michael Smith. Littler was asked to swipe open the pack on FIFA, now known as EA Sports FC.

And he helped the fan get Dominic Berardi's 89-rated special winter wildcard, leaving them ecstatic. They put their hand over their eyes as they showed their phone to the camera.

Littler himself had been enjoying some of the video game after a rare break in what has been a hectic start to 2024 for him. Explaining what he'd done with his week off, he told Sky Sports: "You know me, Xbox and chill!

"But I came here yesterday and then UK Open is on the weekend, so hopefully I can take a win into the UK Open. If not then pick some more points up."

Littler certainly wasn't put off by the impromptu pack opening, getting off to a blistering start against Smith. He averaged just shy of 107 in a 6-2 victory.

He's still on the hunt for his first nightly victory though, losing 6-3 to Rob Cross in their semi-final. He remains in a solid position in the table after impressive performances in recent weeks, including losing in the final of night two to Van Gerwen in Berlin.

The league-leading Dutchman, meanwhile, was convincingly beat 6-3 by reigning world champion Luke Humphries in his quarter-final, giving the rest of the field a chance to hunt down his eight-point lead.
