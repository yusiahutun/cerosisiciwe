Early life

Gemma Arterton was born on 2 February 1986 (age 36 as of 2022) at North Kent Hospital in Gravesend, Kent, England. She is the daughter of Sally Anne Heap (mother) and Barry J. Arterton (father). Her mother runs a cleaning business, while her father is a welder.

Gemma’s sister, Hanna Arterton, is an actress born on 26 January 1989 (age 33 as of 2022) in Gravesend, Kent, England. She has appeared in The Five (2016 TV series) as D.C. Gemma grew up on a council estate in Gravesend. She attended Gravesend Grammar School for Girls, where she discovered her passion for acting. Arterton even made her amateur stage debut in The Boy Who Fell Into A Book and attended many local festivals.

After graduating from high school at 16, Gemma went to an acting college at the Miskin Theatre at North West Kent College. Then, she went to the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London to complete her drama studies. Gemma graduated in 2008 from RADA and became a full-time actress.

Professional career

Gemma started her career in the film industry in 2007. Her first appearance was in the 2007 film St Trinian's, portraying Kelly Jones. She then landed a second role in the film Three and Out the same year, portraying Frankie Cassidy and reprised the same role in 2009.

In 2008, Gemma became a Bond Girl when she played Strawberry Fields in Quantum of Solace. Her outstanding performance in the film won her the Empire Award for Best Newcomer.

In 2010, Arterton appeared in two of the best movies of 2010, including Clash of Titans as Io and Prince of Persia: The Sands of Time as Princess Tamina. Both roles made her receive a Teen Choice Award nomination.
