Tina Fey biography

Tina Fey was born on May 18, 1970, in Upper Darby Township, Pennsylvania, in the United States of America to parents Donald Henry Fey and Zenobia Jeanne Fey. Her mother worked in a brokerage firm while her father wrote grants for the University of Pennsylvania.

She has mixed ethnicity. Her mother is Greek, and her father had German, Northern Irish, and English ancestry. She grew up alongside her brother Peter Fey. Tina Fey joined Beverly Hills middle school before going to Upper Darby High School, where she was the co-editor of the school newspaper.

Tina Fey graduated from high school in 1988 and enrolled at the University of Virginia, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Drama.

Career

Tina had an interest in comedy when she was still in middle school. She debuted her professional career in comedy started when she relocated to Chicago after graduating from the university. She worked as a receptionist during the day at the YMCA in Evanston and took comedy classes at Second City, a training ground for comedians at night.

When was Tina Fey a cast member on SNL?

The biggest break in her career came in 1997 when she submitted several scripts to NBC's variety show Saturday Night Live (SNL), and she was hired as a writer. In 2000, she began performing in sketches, and she and Jimmy Fallon became co-anchors of SNL's Weekend Update segment.

In 2004 Fallon was replaced by Amy Poehler, and for the first time, the weekend update was hosted by two women. The two got a positive response from the public. She worked with SNL from 1997-2006.

At the time of departure, she had co-hosted 117 episodes of the show, which made her the longest-serving anchor at the time. She also made several subsequent SNL guest appearances.
