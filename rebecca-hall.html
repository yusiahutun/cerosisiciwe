Rebecca Hall - Age, Net Worth, Height, Bio, Career, Married, Facts

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, April 10, 2024

Table of Biography

Rebecca Hall is an English actress and film director. Rebecca Hall is known for the British Academy Television Award for Best Supporting Actress for 2009 & has worked on some box office hit films like ‘Prestige’, ‘Vicky Cristina Barcelona’, ‘And Iron Man 3’.

Early Life

Rebecca Maria Hall was born on May 3, 1982, in London, England. Rebecca Hall is currently at the age of 41. Her nickname is Becky. She is the daughter of film director, Peter Hall  & Opera singer, Maria Ewing. But at the age of 8, her parents got divorced.

Furthermore, She has five half-siblings; stage director  Edward Hall, producer Christopher Hall, set designer Lucy Hall, actress Jennifer Carol Hall & Emma Hall. 

Her ethnicity is of mixed ancestry; English, African-American, Dutch, and German. Her nationality is American and British.

Education

She began her education by enrolling in Roedean School and managed to be the head girl. Afterward, she joined St.Catherine’s College, Cambridge to study English literature.

Likewise, She performed actively in student theater scenes and some productions alongside housemate Dan Stevens. Moreover, She was also a member of the Marlowe Society. However, in 2002, she dropped out before her final year.

Career and Professional Life

She got her debut in acting as a young Sophy in her father’s TV adaptation ‘Of the Camomile Lawn’ in 1992. Following that in 2006, her feature film Starter for Ten was released. She had her breakthrough as Sarah Borden in ‘The Prestige’.

Caption: Rebecca Hall in Starter for 10 Source: www.imdb.com

Furthermore, in 2008, she acted as one of the title characters, Vicky in ‘Vicky Cristina Barcelona’. It is one of her best-performing films. She has acted in many blockbuster films throughout her acting career like Iron Man, Godzilla vs Kong, The Night House, and many more.

Accordingly, She had her debut in theatre in 2002 as Vivie in her father’s production at the Strand Theatre in London. In the following years, she continued to act in several plays like As You Like It, and The Fight for Barbara.

Awards and Nominations

Rebecca Hall has been nominated a total of 63 times for various awards not only in acting but in stage performance as well & she has managed to win 11 of them.

Some of the awards she won are Best Supporting Actress for ‘Red Riding: in the Year of our lord’, and ‘The Silver Hugo Award for Best Actress’ for Christine.
