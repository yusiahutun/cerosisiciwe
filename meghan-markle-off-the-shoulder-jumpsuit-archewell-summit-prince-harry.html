The Duchess of Sussex looks chic in white for NYC event

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, March 28, 2024

The Duchess of Sussex looked radiant in an all-white ensemble at the first-ever Archewell Foundation Parents’ Summit, which took place in New York on Tuesday evening.

To co-host the event with husband Harry, Meghan showed up in an ensemble that consisted of a sophisticated off-the-shoulder top with buttons down the centre, plus a pair of matching wide-leg trousers. She capped off the dreamy look with beige suede pumps, a delicate gold chain necklace with a black centre pendant, and tiny gold hoop earrings.

Meanwhile, the prince wore a dark grey suit with a light blue button-down and a black tie.

Bryan Bedder//Getty Images

The summit is part of the charity Project Healthy Minds’ second annual World Mental Health Day Festival, will provide a platform for families who want to create a safer online world and a community that prioritises the mental health of children and teenagers. Families who have suffered tragic losses as a result of their children’s social media usage have been working with the Archewell Foundation over the past year to create solutions, share their experiences, and develop relevant data and research.

"As parents, though our kids are really young – they're two and a half and four and a half – social media is not going away," Meghan said during the event. "I think by design, there was an entry point that was supposed to be positive in creating community and something has devolved, and there's no way to hear that and not try to help these families have their stories be heard."

Bryan Bedder//Getty Images

She also spoke about her fears around social media and how important being a mother to Archie and Lilibet is to her.

"Being a mom is the most important thing in my entire life... outside of course being a wife to this one, but I will say, I feel fortunate that our children are at an age, again, quite young, so this isn't in our immediate future, but I also feel frightened by how it's continuing to change.

"And this will be in front of us. As they say, 'Being a parent, the days are long but the years are short'. So it worries me, but I'm also given a lot of hope and a lot of energy by the progress we've made in the last year."

Bryan Bedder//Getty Images

Earlier in the day, the Sussexes made a pit stop at the Marcy Lab School in Brooklyn, a tuition-free post-secondary education programme that focuses on engineering and technology. There, they also led a conversation about mental health concerns in the digital world.
