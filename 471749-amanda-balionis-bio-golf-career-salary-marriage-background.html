She took golf lessons and even competed at the junior level. The love and knowledge for the sport she developed would be useful in her sports journalism career. Growing up in Lancaster, Amanda attended Manheim Township High School. After graduating, she enrolled at Kutztown University.

She studied at the college for two years and played volleyball for the varsity team. Later, she transferred to Hofstra University, a private university in New York, where she completed her studies, graduating with a degree in broadcast journalism.

Career

Amanda’s journalism career started in college. She worked at the student newspaper when attending Hofstra. Later, she interned for several news organizations such as C.B.S. 2, New York Islanders, A.B.C. News NOW, and New York Jets.

After graduating, she started working at a local newspaper in her hometown, Lancaster. After that, she got a job at Verizon Channel 1, covering high school sports. From Verizon, she moved to M.S.G. Network, which also involved covering high school sports, including being a colour commentator for volleyball competitions.

She also conducted interviews with professional golfers. After working on the tour for several years, she was signed by the sports manufacturing company Callaway Golf as an in-house reporter.

At the company, she helped produce digital content featuring the golf sport. In 2017, Amanda got her big break as a journalist when she signed on with the C.B.S. network as a part-time golf reporter. A year later, she was hired full-time and has worked for the network since then, mostly covering golfing events.

Amanda has occasionally worked as a sideline reporter for C.B.S. during college football games and N.F.L. events.
