'Tinder for hookers' going GLOBAL as swipe-to-sex app to change face prostitution

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, January 26, 2024

Daily Star Online can reveal that Smooci – the number one booking service in Bangkok – is hoping to roll out “around the world”.

It comes after the app's chief revealed they are ready to launch Smooci in London to coincide with Valentine's Day.

Smooci boss Kal Kingsley told Daily Star Online the brand wants to make a “positive change” to the sex work industry – and called current practices “out of date”.

He added they hope apps like Smooci will help break down the stigma around sex work.

The app prides itself on extra levels of safety for the escorts – who have the power to pick and choose their bookings.

Kal said: "We want to become well established around the world and be recognised as a platform which brings greater safety, reliability, and trust.

"We genuinely want to create positive change and use technology to influence this industry more positivity.

“Prostitution is know as the oldest profession in the world, but the ways and methods by which genuine and escorts can meet are seriously outdate.

"We want to use modern tech to bring in new standards, rewarding the genuine clients and escorts, and build a platform around their needs.”

Smooci users can see escorts' live availability and allows them to connect with an online friend in “real time”.

Escorts are given full control – and are said to be allowed to “set their own prices, pictures and services”.

The sex workers can also turn down any bookings they wish, and view their clients to make sure they are safe – as well as plans for escorts to have a safety check-in feature.

Users and sex workers can also leave comments on each and leave ratings – as well as having GPS viewing so they can see where each other are.

Kal said they are also taking extra precautions to make sure the app isn’t abused – monitoring “rogue accounts” to catch out pimps and other undesirables.

He told Daily Star Online: "The more we can do to create greater safety, trust, and verification, should not just help those in the industry, but it should help to create a better image to those on the outside looking in.

"Sex workers have a lot to contend with regarding the legal and safety aspects of their work, social stigma only makes it hard for them to reach out for help and acceptance, adding to the dangers and pressure of their work.”

He said services like Smooci that help make sex work safer and more accessible will help combat problems associated with the industry like human trafficking – creating a safe space for escorts to operate with having to delve into the “dangerous and uncertain” underworld.

Europe is now next on the cards for Smooci, and while the vision is global – the US is not yet in its sights.

"Since the closures of Backpage and Craigslist's 'casual encounters' we have been getting a lot of requests from clients and escorts for us to launch out there,” Kal said.

"But we are currently fully focussed on expanding in to Europe and we see each launch as a big project, which makes it very hard for us to plan beyond that.

"One thing we don't want to do is roll out everywhere and worry about regional challenges and legal issues later. For each expansion to a new country or new city, we want to take the time to study the needs and complication of that area and make the tweaks and improvements to build the best platform possible.

"We also try to build close local industry relationships. If the right industry partners approached us we are always open to conversation, but at the moment we aren't yet looking in to a US launch.”
