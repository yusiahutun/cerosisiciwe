New Mexico author of books on Native American spirituality sues hospital for resuscitating her

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, August 2, 2024

A New Mexico author of books about the Native American spirituality and healing is suing the hospital for causing her heart attack - and saving her as well.

Jamie Sams, 67, known for writing books on Native American medicine and spirituality, filed a lawsuit this week in New Mexico state district court against Santa Fe's Christus St. Vincent Regional Medical Center, according to The Albuquerque Journal.

The suit says the hospital and Dr. Jamie Gagan, who treated her, are to blame for her cardiac arrest because she was given the painkiller, Dilaudid, even after she told staff she was allergic to it.

Jamie Sams, author of Native American medicine and spirituality, did not wish to be resuscitated after a cardiac arrest and is suing the hospital that saved her

She also contends the hospital's negligence was compounded when personnel resuscitated her - contradicting her 'Double DNR (do not resuscitate)' form.

Allegedly, she even wore a purple bracelet with 'DNR' issued by the hospital, according to the lawsuit.

'While we cannot comment on any patient information, CHRISTUS St. Vincent strives to deliver and provide health care that is appropriate to each patient’s unique needs,' hospital spokesperson, Arturo Delgado said in a statement sent to DailyMail.com. 'CHRISTUS St. Vincent maintains a robust Advanced Directives policy that includes forms expressing a patient’s desire to not be resuscitated or intubated. '

Christus St. Vincent Regional Medical Center in Santa Fe, New Mexico, where Jamie Sams had a cardiac arrest and was revived

Sams' last message to readers on her website states 'laughter is healing and the world changes one heart at a time, open yours and walk in beauty'

Listed as a holy person of Cherokee of Seneca descent on the website Native Americans Online, she is said to describe indigenous people's approach to dying being like a bow, and people are the arrows.

Her website Jamiesamsbooks.com lists seven books she authored or co-authored on topics of Native American wisdom, such as 'Earth Medicine: Ancestor's Ways of Harmony for Many Moons' and 'Medicine Cards: The Discovery of Power Through the Ways of Animals.'

She also collaborated with musician John York, former member of The Byrds, on an album entitled 'Clan Mother Songs,' that features 'ancient chants of the clan mothers for every child of the Earth.'

'Medicine Cards' co-authored by Sams is about the 'the evolving life-lessons that we need to embrace as human beings facing the new millennium and our continued healing processes.

Jamie Sams website displaying her books and audio recordings

The Albuquerque Journal reports that Sams also suffers from the 'extremely debilitating and painful' disease, Dercum's, an illness that produces fatty, benign tumors to grow upon the body.   

A letter to her readers in 2016 announces the closure of two medicine-related websites she had been running called Children of Earth and Emerging Worlds due to Internet costs and the volume of 'requests for alternative healing methods and source material to get started in every individual's situation.'

'It became overwhelming for me since I was the one personally answering most questions and referring people to holistic and ceremonial methods of healing to be used along with medical care,' Sams wrote at the time.
