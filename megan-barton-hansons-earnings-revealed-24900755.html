Megan Barton-Hanson's earnings revealed after raking it in on OnlyFans

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, September 25, 2024

Megan Barton-Hanson became the ultimate sex symbol on series four of Love Island after coming in as a bombshell and stealing Laura Anderson's man.

In her iconic teasing moments when she lured Wes Nelson away from Laura, Megan captivated the nation at home and has managed to keep the spotlight on her since.

She has now found success on content sharing platform OnlyFans, where subscribers pay for her racy uploads for a monthly fee.

This has led to her making a fortune alongside her multiple endorsement deals.

The stunner, 27, reportedly has more than £400,000 in the bank, due to her healthy load of OnlyFans subscribers.

This has also been contributed to by deals with popular brands, as well as appearances on reality TV shows such as C4's Celebs Go Dating.

Megan previously worked as a stripper before appearing on the ITV2 dating show in 2018, where she became central to the show's main storylines.

The star filed the latest accounts for her firm MBH Productions Ltd, which were then obtained by MailOnline, showing the whopping figures.

Since leaving the villa, Megan has landed sought-after brand deals with companies such as PrettyLittleThing, which she often promotes to her Instagram followers.

Over the past few years, she has also teamed up with lingerie brand Ann Summers and released her own range of saucy underwear, Megan Loves, and also stands as the saucy firm’s Director of the Pleasure Positivity Project.

In her first year of business, she reportedly earned more than £250,000, and according to her latest accounts up to 31 August 2020, MBH Productions Ltd had £398,125 in the bank, with over a quarter of a million pounds in reserve.

Megan then chose to return to OnlyFans in March last year, when the UK went into lockdown, and now rakes in thousands sharing for explicit videos and selfies on the website.

Due to this, she was able to purchase her first home in Essex last month and has plans to buy more properties to rent out.

Megan also recently launched a sex-positive podcast, called You Come First, which is also set to boost her bank balance.

This month OnlyFans made the decision to ban sexually explicit content on their website, which Megan was enraged about, leading her to stand up fellow sex workers.

It has been reported that Megan now earns over £800,000-a-month from the members-only website, so it makes sense why she was quick to speak against the platform's decision.

She told i newspaper: “I can’t say I’m not disappointed. I have always backed OnlyFans, I felt it was amazing to see a site that celebrated allowing people to do what they want with their bodies.

“OnlyFans has also been a really safe and effective way to earn a taxable income, and I am definitely worried content creators might resort to more dangerous ways to make money if this income is at risk."
