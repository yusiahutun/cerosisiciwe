Dehiwala-Mount Lavinia is a vibrant coastal suburb of Colombo.

Situated on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, this bustling city offers breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean and a serene beachfront.

It is the largest suburb of Colombo.

With a population of over 200,000 residents, Dehiwala-Mount Lavinia is a thriving hub of residential, commercial, and recreational activities.

Dehiwala-Mount Lavinia has a rich colonial history.

The area was once under Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonial rule, leaving behind a legacy of architectural landmarks and cultural influences.

The city is home to the National Zoological Gardens of Sri Lanka.

The famous Mount Lavinia beach is located in Dehiwala-Mount Lavinia.

Stretching over 1.6 kilometers, this golden sandy beach is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, offering opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, and beachside dining.
