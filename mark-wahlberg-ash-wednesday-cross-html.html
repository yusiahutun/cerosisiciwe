Mark Wahlbergproudly shows off the Ash Wednesday cross on his forehead while arriving to the GMA S

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, June 19, 2024

Mark Wahlberg kicked off the morning bright and early, in order to make it to church before appearing on various morning talk shows in New York City on Wednesday. 

As evidenced by the ashes placed in the shape of a cross on his forehead, the 52-year-old actor, who is a devout Catholic, partook in the Christian tradition that marks the start of the holy season of Lent, before making his appearance on Good Morning America. 

While making his way into the studio, the father-of-four sported a navy windbreaker, dark-wash jeans and white sneakers. 

In addition to promoting his upcoming film, Arthur the King, which comes out next month, he has been hard at work promoting his app, Hallow, which offers audio-guided meditation sessions to help users grow their faith and find peace in God.

Earlier in the day, he and actor Jonathan Roumie appeared on Fox & Friends to speak about Hallow and Lent, which begins on February 14, 2024. 

'People are always asking, what are you giving up? I've given many things up for Lent, whether it be… alcohol or something… sweet, but for me, it's really about trying to do more… trying to be better, trying to grow as a person, as a servant,' Wahlberg said during the appearance. 

Wahlberg also made time to share a Valentine's Day tribute to his longtime wife, Rhea Durham, on Instagram. 

'Happy Valentine’s Day, babe,' he captioned a loved-up snap of them together as he wrapped one arm around her shoulders. 

The image showed them both smiling directly at the camera as she donned apink sweater with 'love' on it and a pair of light-wash blue jeans. 

In October 2022, the couple relocated to Las Vegas with their kids Ella, 20, Michael, 17, Brendan, 15 and Grace, 13. 

'Everybody's adapted nicely. The kids are all out at school, and everybody's happy,' Wahlberg said. 

He added that he's, 'constantly busy and traveling, so I'm looking forward to getting home and spending some time with the family' over the holidays.

Wahlberg revealed in a mid-October 2022 appearance on The Talk that he moved his family to Nevada.

'I want to be able to work from home. I moved to California many years ago to pursue acting and I've only made a couple of movies in the entire time that I was there,' he said.

'To be able to give my kids a better life and follow and pursue their dreams, whether it be my daughter as an equestrian, my son as a basketball player, my younger son as a golfer, this made a lot more sense for us,' he added.

'We came here to just kind of give ourselves a new look, a fresh start for the kids, and there's lot of opportunity here,' he added.

'I'm really excited about the future,' he added.

'Hopefully my kids, we’ll see what their interests are, but I don’t think that I’ll be acting that much longer at the pace I am now. That’s for sure. Because that’s the most difficult thing,' Wahlberg added.
