Career

Luis Arraez signed as an international free agent with Minnestone Twins in November 2013. He had an impressive record in the minor league of .331/.385/ .414 hitter in 367 games.

In 2015, as Luis made his debut with the Dominican Summer League Twins, he had hit  .348/.433/.400/.833 with 15 RBI. The same year, as he played with GCL Twins, he recorded .306/.377/.388/.765 with 19 RBI.

Following it, in the next year, 2016, Arraez played along with the Cedar Rapids Kernels. During it, he had hit  .347/.386/.444/.830 with 3 home runs and 66 RBI.

In 2017, Luis could only attend three games for the Fort Myers Miracle because of his torn ACL (Anterior cruciate ligament injury). After his recovery, for the 2018 season, Arraez played with the Fort Myers and the Chattanooga Lookouts.

He recorded a combined .310/.361/.397/.758 with 3 home runs and 36 RBI. As the season ended, Luis was added to the 40-man roster by the Twins.

Simultaneously, Luis started his 2019 season with the Pensacola Blue Wahoos, recording .342/.415/.397/.812 with 14 RBI in 38 games. On May 14, he was promoted to the Rochester Red Wings.

Following it on May 17, he was selected for the major leagues, and thus, on May 18, he made his debut playing against the Seattle Mariners. He made his first career hit during the game, gaining a double-off Mariners pitcher Cory Gearrin. 

Similarly, on May 21, 2019, Luis Arraez made his first career major league home run against the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim. He ranked sixth in American League Rookie of the Year as he closed the season with a record of .334 in 92 games.
