Career and sources of income

Donald began his career immediately after completing his university studies. He practised law briefly in the 1970s. Later, he quit law and joined his father in the business sector. He became part of the Sugar Foods Corp company.

He brought significant growth to the company by turning its flagship product, Sweet N' Low, into a mainstay on many restaurant tables and kitchen counters across the United States. Who inherited Donald Tober’s fortune?

His wife, Barbara Tober, inherited Donald Tober's fortune, valued at $30 million. Long before his death, Donald had filed his will in Manhattan Surrogate's Court on 5th October 2018. Donald left Barbara his entire estate.

I give the balance of my estate … to my wife if she survives me.

Barbara also inherited the 11th-floor Park Avenue apartment where they lived, including the furniture, artwork, and cars. Donald also specified that all his stock in Sugar Foods, which stopped its distribution more than 15 years ago, should be sold off as the proceeds given to Barbara.

Distribute to my wife outright all proceeds of such sale and place any promissory notes from the sale in a trust for the exclusive benefit of my wife.
