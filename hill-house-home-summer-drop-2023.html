Hill House Homes Newest Dresses Are Summer 2023 Must-Haves

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, March 25, 2024

When Hill House Home announces an upcoming collection to its devout followers, founder and CEO Nell Diamond says the Instagram comments feel more like a group chat—the type where notifications ping by the second and heart-eye emojis are sent with abandon. This year’s Hill House summer drop, arriving today at noon, nearly made the so-called group chat crash with excitement over its initial reveal.

Hill House HomeRefreshed dress silhouettes, like the Seraphina Dress ($225), combine Hill House’s signature smocking with elevated prints.Hill House HomeAnother new Hill House Home style, the semi-sheer Enzo Dress ($195)

With more than 50 pieces—including 18 new dresses—Hill House Home’s summer lineup is the biggest collection the brand has ever released. A corresponding photo shoot set at a sprawling, oceanside Palm Beach estate sets a Slim Aarons tone for the lineup of matching sets, cover-ups, and dresses of all lengths. These are clothes for a summer of luxuriating in the highest form, without losing the brand’s signature wearability.

The group chat’s reaction? “There’s too much to process,” one shopper responded.

A Few of Our Favorite New Dresses

The ruffles, flounces, and floral prints of previous Hill House Home collections are all present and accounted for—trimmings for a Cape Cod summer, complete with a basket bag lined with coordinating fabric. Dedicated shoppers will notice the collection explores aesthetics that feel entirely new for the label (even if existing customers had already styled their dresses in a similar fashion). Black jersey dresses, from a sleeveless mini to an off-the-shoulder maxi, will speak to nap-dress-curious minimalists. Eyelet crop-top and shorts separates in a poppy red and midi dresses with a flouncy drop waist have a distinctly European vacation vibe. Dresses and cropped cardigans in a sexy semi-sheer knit could become the unofficial fabric of summer bachelorette trips. Design-wise, it feels like Hill House Home is in its abundance era—and that’s intentional.

Hill House Home

The collection includes bestsellers like the Ellie Nap Dress ($150) in a summer stripe print.

“Over the years, we’ve coined the term ‘Nap Dress Summer’ and have continued to release bigger drops each year, so this collection being our largest in history truly came naturally,” Diamond explains. “[W]hen we’ve tested new silhouettes and fabrics in the past, they have been very well received, so we had the confidence and learnings to do even more.”

Growth is also a general theme at Hill House these days. The label is getting ready to open a Charleston, South Carolina store later this summer, its fourth location; it’s also kickstarting dedicated operations in the United Kingdom.

Beyond business metrics like stores opened and dresses sold, expanding well beyond core nap-dress styles has special significance for the brand. Hill House’s universe was always supposed to be wider than the pastoral vibes associated with its first nap dresses; this collection confirms it.

Hill House Home

“Personally, I’ve always resisted the idea of being all one thing—even in the way I dress,” Diamond says. “Sometimes I want an over-the-top pink-tulle-and-glitter moment, and other times I’m excited to channel my inner Morticia Addams in all black and a great pair of sunglasses. No one style represents Hill House, in the same way that there is no specific Hill House girl; there’s room for everyone at the table.”

Today’s summer drop is the brand’s best offering to welcome every aesthetic in the nap-dress multiverse. That doesn’t necessarily make filling up a cart easier when the collection arrives in a few short hours. As one of the label’s dedicated fans opined in the launch sheet comments, “I just … how are we supposed to choose[?]”

Shop the entire summer collection exclusively at Hill House Home, in stores and online, at noon eastern today.
