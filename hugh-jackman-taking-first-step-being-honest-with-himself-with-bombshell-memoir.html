Hugh Jackman taking 'first step of being honest with himself' in 'bombshell memoir'

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, February 18, 2024

Hollywood heartthrob Hugh Jackman is ready to open up candidly about his life in an upcoming memoir, a source claims.

Wolverine star Hugh Jackman is never shy around sharing his truth. The celebrity who was estranged from his mother in childhood opened up about his deep-rooted trauma later in his life recently. And now, fans will get an even closer look at his life with the deeply personal memoir that he has reportedly started writing.

Hugh Jackman taking the ‘first step’

Reports claim Hugh Jackman is writing an up close and personal memoir that will have a fair share of honest truths about his life which have never shared before. Speaking of the book, an insider teased it will have some bombshell facts.

“The content will be Hugh speaking about his life,” says the source. They added: “He’ll be opening up about his life like never before. (So far), It’s been very healing for him.”

Hugh Jackman is currently going through a divorce with his wife Deborra-Lee Furness, 67. The celebrity couple ended their 27 years of marriage this year. The movie star also shares two kids with former wife, Oscar, 23, and Ava, 18. So taking up the writing project has helped Jackman as he takes another step towards being honest with himself.

Hugh Jackman opens up about seeking help

Over the years, more and more Hollywood A-listers have talked about the importance of therapy and destigmatizing mental health and Hugh Jackman is one of the biggest spokespersons of the benefits of therapy.

The 54-year-old celebrity revealed last year therapy helped him deal with his childhood trauma. He said in an interview that he started sessions recently and it has helped him a lot. He added, “having someone really smart, who’s a little bit removed from your world, can be really helpful.”

“Most importantly, It’s helping me to be more relational with the people I love in my life, and really understanding and living in their shoes and being clear to be able to see them,” he gushed.
