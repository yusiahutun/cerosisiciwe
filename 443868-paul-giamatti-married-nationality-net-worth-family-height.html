Paul Giamatti's biography

Paul Edward Valentine Giamatti was born on 6th June 1967 in New Haven, Connecticut, in the United States. His parents are Angelo Barlett Giamatti (father) and Toni Marilyn (mother). He has two siblings, a sister, Elena Giamatti and a brother Marcus Giamatti.

His mother was a former actress who taught at a preparatory school in Connecticut. On the other hand, his father was a professor and president of Yale University. Later, he became the commissioner of Major League Baseball (MLB). His sister is a jewellery designer, while his brother is an actor.

Paul enrolled in The Foote School before joining Choate Rosemary Hall and graduating in 1985. After his high school, he enrolled at Yale University for a Bachelor's Degree in English and graduated in 1989. Later, he earned his Master of Fine Arts Degree from Yale School of Drama.

Career

Paul was an active actor since his college days, where he started appearing in various theatre productions. Several minor roles followed this in the 1990s. He landed his first big role in 1997 in the film, Private Parts. This was followed by roles in The Truman Show, Big Fat Liar, and Saving Private Ryan.

The actor gained critical acclaim after appearing in American Splendor. This was followed by the award-winning romantic comedy titled, Sideways. Finally, he made his action movie debut in Shoot Em Up. In 2012, he became a voiceover actor in the Liberty Mutual Insurance commercial.

Over the years, he has starred in numerous movies and TV shows. He has bagged many awards and nominations with his excellent performance, including four Screen Actor Awards, two Golden Globe Awards, and One Primetime Emmy Awards.
