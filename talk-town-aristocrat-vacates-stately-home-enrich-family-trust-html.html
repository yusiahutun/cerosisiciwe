Aristocrat vacates stately home to enrich family trust

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, January 26, 2024

He's lorded it over a 1,500-acre estate and magnificent 15-bedroom Jacobean stately home for nearly a decade.

But now, after years of assuming ownership of one of England’s finest 17th Century piles – Pylewell Park in Lymington, Hampshire – David Roper- Curzon has volunteered to vacate the sprawling mansion temporarily, as trustees advised the family to use the house as a venue for their private event business in order to enrich the family trust.

The eccentric, twice-married sculptor took sole possession of his family’s ancestral home after it was vacated by his father, Lord Teynham in 2010.

Now David has vacated the property, with the agreement of his 90-year-old papa, to ensure the family seat remains for generations to come.

David Roper- Curzon has volunteered to vacate the sprawling mansion temporarily 

Says a friend of the Roper-Curzon family, which includes pop star Birdy, ‘David’s father has reminded his eldest son that the future of Pylewell Hall depends on launching privates and weddings to keep the trust afloat and ensure the next generations don’t lose the estate which is why they’ve decided to turn it into a wedding venue for the next few years until such time as the trust is in good health’. 

My source said ‘Lord Teynham is very keen to capitalise on the venue’s popularity because he thinks that will ensure it is never sold off.’  

Trustees advised the family to use the house as a venue for their private event business in order to enrich the family trust

According to my source, David is now living in a small house on the estate where he can continue to be close to the sculpture studio he keeps on the grounds.

David, his brother Ben and his sister-in-law Victoria all declined to comment when I approached them last week.

Only in Toffland.

CRUSH OF THE WEEK... Scarlett Wilson 

Actress Scarlett Wilson is back in London after ten years in India starring in Bollywood. After a brief marriage over there she is single again.

Her return hit a stumbling block in the summer when she unwittingly struck up a relationship with a conman. ‘I was very lost in fake people,’ Scarlett, 30, told me at the Groucho Club. ‘I met this guy in Mayfair who wormed his way into my life and turned out to be a criminal. He would go into high-end places and tell them they’d lost his credit card behind the bar. They were so embarrassed they believed him and he would buy the whole bar drinks with no credit card.’

Scarlett would not identify him but I can report he is Medi Abalimba – who hit the headlines in April when he was dumped by Love Island’s Georgia Steel after she found out that in 2014 he had been sentenced to four years for impersonating former Chelsea footballer Gael Kakuta to swindle people out of thousands of pounds.

Things are looking up though – Scarlett is in new film The Last Faust and has been hanging with Gerard Butler. Good for her!
