What to Look For in a Budget Android Phone in 2024

As technology continues to evolve, features once acquainted with flagship phones have since trickled down into more affordable alternatives. This couldn't be any more true than when it comes to the smartphone market.

Nowadays, budget smartphones, particularly budget Android phones, can master the fundamentals of a great smartphone while not draining your bank account. You could get a fantastic phone for a great price, depending on which manufacturer you buy from. You just need to know where to look.

Of course, finding that perfect deal can be tedious with so many different Android options on the market. It can also be confusing if you're unsure which processor, RAM and storage capacities, camera specs, and battery sizes to look out for.

That's where we come in. Below, we've compiled a list of the best budget Android phones you can buy right now, depending on how much you want to spend and which features matter to you. This list covers a range of manufacturers and price points, so you can find what you need.

Best Budget Android Phone Overall: Motorola Moto G Play (2024)

Cons

Budget phones have come a long way, but Motorola has been making solid, affordable phones for a decade now. It may have "play" in the name, but the Motorola Moto G Play (2024) is an all-around solid phone, no matter how you're looking to use it.

You wouldn't think this was a cheap phone looking at the 6.5-inch display, which can reach up to 500 nits of brightness at a 1600x720 resolution. This makes for a pixel density of 269ppi--not the most impressive, but still plenty sharp. The phone also features an IP52 rating, meaning it can withstand some exposure to dust and water.

The phone is powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 640 4G mobile platform, with an Adreno 610 GPU and 4GB RAM. You only get 64GB internal storage, but you can back this up with the microSD slot, which supports cards up to 512GB to storage photos, videos, music, and more.

You get a pair of rear-facing cameras, with the main camera boasting a 50MP quad-pixel sensor. The phone also supports both Dolby Atmos and hi-res audio, though this (as is usually the case with hi-res) is only available via wired headphones. Fortunately, the phone features a 3.5mm headphone jack, so it's easy to plug in your favorite headphones.

While many budget phones use Android releases a few versions older than the current release, the 2024 Moto G Play features Android 13.0. It's available in any color you like, as long as that color is Sapphire Blue.

Battery life can be hard to come by on budget phones. Manufacturers need to make cuts somewhere to come in at their target price, and batteries are often the easiest place to save on costs. If you're looking for maximum battery in an affordable phone, the Motorola Moto G Power 5G (2023) is a great option.

The Moto G Power 5G features a MediaTek Dimensity 930 chipset and 6GB RAM, backed by 256GB storage. This is expandable via the microSD slot, which supports cards up to 512GB. Powering this is a 5,000mAh battery, which you'll find in other phones at this price, but Motorola seems to have worked some magic, as many people get two days of use between charges.

The phone features a 6.5-inch LCD screen with a 120Hz refresh rate to keep things feeling extra smooth. A pair of stereo speakers flanks the screen, with support for Dolby Atmos to add another dimension to music and videos.

On the back of the phone, you'll find a 50MP, dual-lens camera system. While it won't blow flagship cameras out of the water, it's still more than capable. Of course, you also get a front-facing camera for selfies and group photos.

The Moto G Power 5G 2023 is available in Mineral Black, Bright White, and Cosmic Emerald color varieties.

The Samsung Galaxy A54 5G, which is replacing its predecessor—the Galaxy A53 5G—packs all the right stuff for a solid mid-range smartphone. It has a big and beautiful 6.4-inch Super AMOLED screen with an adaptive refresh rate of up to 120Hz. The company has also increased the peak brightness to 1000 nits, which is plenty to keep everything on the screen readable in even bright outdoor conditions.

The 5,000mAh battery also performs admirably and will last through a day, even with heavy usage. You also get fast 25W wired charging support. But sadly, there is still no wireless charging.

Another positive for the phone is its Exynos 1380 SoC, which packs tons of processing power. This results in smooth multitasking and an overall responsive experience. The Exynos chip is also suitable for gaming, and it will easily handle almost every game you throw at it. However, in graphics-intensive games like Genshin Impact, you may have to turn down the graphics settings for a completely lag-free experience.

In other features, the A54's camera performance is good, but certainly not flagship level. You'll be able to capture excellent shots in good lighting conditions with plenty of detail. However, the smartphone struggles in low lighting as it allows visual noise to creep in and takes longer to lock autofocus.

You'll also be happy to know that the phone runs on Android 13 with One UI 5.1 out of the box. And the company has promised to deliver four Android version updates and up to five years of security patches. This is even better than what Google offers with its Pixel phones.

Lastly, as the name suggests, you can enjoy 5G connectivity on the A54 5G. The phone is certified to work with the 5G networks on all three major carriers. But only the Verizon model, which costs $50 more than other versions, supports mmWave 5G.

FAQ

How do you factory reset an Android phone?

A factory reset can ready your phone for trade-in, stomp out pesky bugs, and clear out significant storage space. The process is pretty easy: Tap Settings, select General & Backup, choose Factory data reset, tap Reset device, and then Delete all. If your phone veered off the path at some point, check out Google's support page, which houses links for most Android carriers' help pages.

How do I unlock an Android phone?

Unlocking an Android phone to move providers usually only takes a few steps. The first of which is to contact your carrier and ask them to unlock your phone. Note: if you're still paying off your smartphone, your carrier may not honor the request. Then, if you're using a physical SIM card, replace your old carrier's card; you're good to go!

How do you connect a Bluetooth device to an Android phone?

Connecting a Bluetooth device (say, a pair of wireless earbuds) to your Android phone is simple once you know where to look. Make sure Bluetooth is enabled by swiping down from the top of the screen and tapping the Bluetooth symbol if disabled. Then, touch and hold the same Bluetooth icon. Select the device you want to connect from the list. You may need to put the Bluetooth device in pairing mode using a physical button located somewhere on the exterior.

Can you use an Apple Watch with an Android phone?

Unfortunately no, you cannot use an Apple Watch with an Android phone. On the bright side, there are several Android smartwatch alternatives to choose from, whether you want something that sticks by your side during intense runs or tells you the time and doesn't cost your life savings.
