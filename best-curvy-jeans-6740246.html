The Best Jeans for Curvy Women of 2023

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, March 28, 2024

Finding well-fitted jeans is one of life’s never-ending quests that requires a lot of trial and error, no matter what size or body type you are. For women with curves, whether that be plus-sized body types or curvaceous figures, looking for the right fit and finding extended sizing can sometimes be another add-on to the research checklist. However, once a brand or particular style is found that works, the floodgates are opened. 

On your hunt, you might be puzzled by the terminology retailers use and their sizing qualifications. Model and size-inclusive fashion expert Lauren Chan tells InStyle that curvy-fit denim vastly depends on the brand. “Be sure to read fit notes when it comes to ‘curvy’ denim because sometimes it simply means that the standard-size jeans have been graded up into plus-sizes, but other times it means that the jeans have specifically been designed for curvier folks — i.e., with larger than average waist-to-hip ratios,” she says. 

We tapped a group of four fashion experts to decipher between brands and share their picks for the best denim for curvy women, including a mix of both extended-sized styles and curvy fits designed specifically for those with smaller waists and fuller hips. Let’s hear it for no more waist gap!

What to Keep in Mind

Style

“Style is the number one detail that should be considered when shopping for curvy women because you want to make sure that the denim will look good,” Underwood says. “Most curvy shapes look great in high-waist or mid-rise denim, along with wide-leg denim to help elongate the body and give the illusion of a longer torso.” However, all the experts agree that style should be based on preference. “These days there are no fashion rules and people can really wear whatever they feel most comfortable in,” Blashka says. Go for a style that you love, no matter what.

Composition

Clothing composition is also key Underwood says, when shopping for denim. “As a curvy woman, I stand clear of denim that is 100 percent cotton unless it's a jacket of some sort,” she says. “If there is elastic in the composition of the denim, I know that there’s a chance that the jeans won’t be as comfortable.” Blashka also looks for elasticity. “Stretch is key in a curve jean,” she says. “It allows for maximum comfort with movement, and helps accommodate your shape.” Take this into account while you look through the options. Our top pick, Universal Standard's Etta High Rise Straight Leg, has only a touch of elastane. For a stretch content that mimics shapewear, on the other hand, we recommend Amazon Essentials' Women's Mid Rise Curvy Skinny Jean or Spanx's Crop Kick Flare style,

And although many appreciate the qualities, not everyone is a fan of stretch denim. “There are a few brands that offer more of a limited stretch, which I prefer,” says Li. “I strongly dislike jeans that grow with you. The cut can sometimes look unflattering after a while or by the end of the day.” Chan is also on the non-stretch team. “Most of the time, curvy and plus-size jeans are made with stretch fabrics because as people are larger in size, their bodily projections vary more drastically. Some folks, like me, are widest through the mid-section, and others are widest through the hip,” she explains. Although it's a rare find, there are non-stretch, 100-percent cotton options out there.

Body Type

“If you google body types, there’s a chance you’ll see close to 12 different body types,” Underwood says. “In the case of shopping for curvy women, it's important to know what body type you’re shopping for so that you can accentuate and highlight the right things.” Knowing your measurements will also help narrow down the options for your best fit. Read the notes on each style to help find the best for you. 

Your Questions, Answered

What does a curvy-fit mean?

There is some discrepancy when it comes to what a curvy fit actually is. As Chan mentioned above, it greatly depends on what brand you’re looking at and how its sizing and language are quantified. Many assume that a curvy fit is a cut with a smaller waist-to-hip ratio, or one that provides more room in the hips, thighs, and butt.

“Curvy fit does not necessarily mean plus-size,” says Blashka. “Curvy fits typically have smaller waists (so there is less gapping at the waist), and more room in the seat, hips, and thighs and sometimes more room in the calf area as well.” She recommends curvy fits for hourglass or pear-shaped bodies.

What is the best way to care for denim?

All our experts have different washing styles. “I know some people like to dry clean their denim, but personally I wash on cold or warm inside out and air dry,” says Blashka. “Sometimes I’ll put them in the dryer for a few minutes or steam them to get the stiffness out.” She does discourage using high heat dry in case of potential shrinking. In terms of frequency, Underwood recommends limiting washes as much as possible. “A tip I follow is washing my denim after two or three wears to prolong the quality. Washing your denim on a gentle or delicate cycle and using color-preserving detergent is key to keeping the vibrancy of the denim,” she says. “Also, make sure that you lay your jeans flat to air dry instead of using the dryer.”

Chan has another tip for readers: “I’ve heard that it’s to put your jeans in the freezer to ‘clean’ them by freezing bacteria but I stick to a good ol’ wash and hang dry — more room for frozen pizza that way,” she says. Of course, consulting the care label is another way to care for your denim.
