Reuben Kigame Pays Moving Tribute to Joe Kayo, Recounts His 2006 Visit After Wife's Death

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, July 10, 2024
  • Joe Kayo is celebrated as the founder of Deliverance Churches in Kenya and Uganda and the pioneer of the charismatic Pentecostal movement in East Africa
  • Reuben Kigame reminisced Kayo, whom he had not known in person, and drove from Nairobi to Eldoret to commiserate with him after his wife Mercy Wanderwa's death
  • The gospel singer wrote a moving tribute to Kayo, narrating how the encounter touched his life and fulfilled the ministry of presence as taught by CITAM Bishop Emeritus David Oginde

Michael Ollinga is a journalist at TUKO.co.ke with over 10 years of experience covering courts and crimes, special reports, and current affairs in Kenya.

Celebrated gospel musician, politician, and author Reuben Kigame has sent a moving tribute to the family of the founder of the Deliverance Churches in Kenya and Uganda, Apostle Joe Kayo.

Kayo, who is widely recognised as the father of charismatic Pentecostalism in East Africa, breathed his last on Thursday, November 2, aged 86.

Many of Kayo's proteges, spiritual sons, daughters, and congregations had good words for the fallen general of the gospel of Christ, recounting personal or corporate encounters with him that transformed their lives.

Taking to his social media accounts, Kigame vividly remembered how Kayo, whom he had not known in person, visited his Eldoret home to condole him following the death of his (Kigame's) wife, Mercy Wanderwa Kigame.

Reuben Kigame opens up about encounter with Joe Kayo

Mercy succumbed after being involved in a fatal road accident on September 5, 2006. She was taking their daughter, Shema, to a hospital in Nairobi before the unfortunate incident around the Kabarak area.

Kigame said that his encounter with Kayo was personal and one that would never cease to exist in his memory.

He said that many people had good things and several others had not-so-good things to say about Kayo, but his tribute was beyond his eloquent teaching, miracles, or the mistakes he made.

"Mine is about one afternoon towards the end of the year 2006 when a stranger drove through the gate of my home in Eldoret, and I was told I had a visitor. It was Apostle Dr Joe Kayo, the "father of Pentecostalism" in Africa," Kigame narrated.

According to Kigame, Kayo had driven from Nairobi to Eldoret for one thing: to pray and sympathise with him and his family.

"We have not met, and you may not know me, but I am Joe Kayo. I know you and deeply appreciate your ministry to me and the Body of Christ in this country and worldwide. I have just come to tell you and your family 'pole sana' (sorry) for the loss of your wife.

"I want to encourage you to be strong and continue despite the pain. We are praying for you as a family, and I know many saints who are praying for you to stand," Kigame, a presidential aspirant in the 2022 General Election, recounted.

To Kigame, Kayo's visit manifested the teachings of Christ Is The Answer Ministries (CITAM) Bishop Emeritus Dr. David Oginde.

"It is what Bishop Emeritus Dr. David Oginde called in one of his sermons "the ministry of presence." He touched lives differently. This is how he touched my life. Farewell, great general. Sleep tight till I see you on resurrection morning," Kigame added.

Kayo has mentored many proteges who have risen to become great preachers of the gospel of Christ.
