Besides, he has had short periods at Oakland Raiders and New England Patriots. Before beginning his NFL career in 2013, he played collegiate football with the Tennessee Volunteers football team.

Cordarrelle Patterson: Early Life, Family, and Education

In fact, Patterson was born in Rock Hill, South Carolina, on March 17, 1991.

Besides, he was raised by his single mother, Catherine, with his older brother and sister, Charles and Crystal. Soon, he joined Northwestern High School in Rock Hill, South Carolina.

Not to mention, Patterson was involved in football, basketball, and track running in his high school days.

As a matter of fact, he had 75 passes caught with 18 touchdowns for 944 yards in his senior year in 2008.

In the meantime, he had led the school team to the State Class 4A Division II championship.

Notably, he made it to the All-State team while being a letterman in track and field for three years.

Furthermore, he stayed at North Carolina Tech Preparatory Christian Academy for a while after graduation for a post-graduation session.

Cordarrelle Patterson: College Career

Hutchinson Community College

Football at Hutchinson

Later, Patterson switched to Hutchinson Community College in Kansas, joining the junior college football team there. In no time, he made it to the NJCAA All-American team in 2010 and 2011.

Overall he has 1832 receiving yards, 113 receptions, 24 receiving touchdowns, 36 total touchdowns, and 42.9 kickoff return averages for the team, all of which are the record stats.

In fact, he was titled with the return specialist honor in his freshman year, where he had 21 combined kickoff and punt returns.

Furthermore, he had been selected for the All-American first-team as an offensive player. Notably, he had received the Jayhawk Conference Offensive Player of the Year in 2011.

Involvement in Track and field

Besides football, Patterson had interests in track & field too. Notably, he had completed the 100-meter dash in 10.33 seconds and 200-meter in 21.19 seconds, which were his personal bests at the Jayhawk Conference Championship.

Furthermore, he took part in the long jump, recording a collegiate-best of 6.85 meters jump at the Southwestern Invitational Championships.

He had also clocked 40.32 seconds in his part of a 4×100-meter relay at the Levelland Meet in May 2011.

Tennessee University

No doubt, Patterson’s shine had reached all over the nation during his record-breaking days at Hutchinson.

Accordingly, he had numerous offers from Division I programs of different colleges. Eventually, he decided to join the University of Tennessee, joining the Volunteers football team in 2012.

Most of the time, Patterson played as a wide receiver for the team while showing his versatility as a running back and return specialist.

Similarly, he had 93 receiving yards with six receptions and a receiving touchdown against North Carolina State in a 35-21 season opener victory.

Overall, he set the college’s record for the most all-purpose yards (1,858) topped the Southeastern Conference.

In fact, he recorded at least one receiving touchdown, rush, kick return, and punt return in 12 games for the Volunteers.

Ultimately, he made an early exit from college, declaring for the NFL draft in January 2013 with few other teammates.

Cordarrelle Patterson: Professional Career

Minnesota Vikings

Draft and the debut

Soon, the Minnesota Vikings poached Patterson in the first round of the 2013 NFL Draft with a draft-day trade with the New England Patriots.

Similarly, his regular-season debut came in the season opener, where he had a 10-yard gain by catching a pass by Christian Ponder, Samantha Ponder’s husband.

Also, the first NFL touchdown came for him against the Chicago Bears, consisting of Eben Britton.

In fact, he made it to the NFC Teams Player of the Month in September 2013. Overall, he made 45 catches in his rookie season, covering 469 yards with five touchdowns.

Decline in performance

As a matter of fact, his second in NFL could not bring him the joy he sought. With new head coach Mike Zimmer and rookie quarterback Teddy Bridgewater, Patterson could not provide his performance level.

He was also out of the starting position while having caught only 33 passes for 384 yards with one touchdown.

However, he reclaimed his position in the starting lineup soon after the wide receiver Greg Jennings was out of the team.

Again, he acquired the fifth position to start as the club signed new wide receivers. After all, he had only two receptions in the 2015 season with two kick returns.

Final season with the Vikings

Not to mention, he found himself more associated with the kick returner job rather than the wide receiver in next season too.

On the whole, he received 52 receptions for 453 yards with two receiving touchdowns too in the season that would later be his last with the Vikings. Furthermore, he recorded 25 kickoff returns.

Oakland Raiders

As a matter of fact, Patterson signed for Oakland Raiders with a two-year, $8.5 million contract in March 2017.

Furthermore, he debuted for them in the 2017 season opener, where he had one five-yard rush and one two-yard reception playing as a kickoff returner.

Furthermore, Patterson had a touchdown for 47 yards against Phillip Rivers’ Los Angeles Chargers in Week 6, where the Raiders suffered a 17-16 loss.

Overall, he had 31 receptions for 309 yards with 13 carries for 121 yards, including a couple of touchdowns for the Raiders.

New England Patriots

Next season, the Raiders traded him to the New England Patriots for a fifth-round pick of March’s 2018 season.

After all, his debut for the Patriots came with a 27-20 victory over the Houston Texans in the season opener. Besides, he had played as a second running back against the Green Bay Packers in Week 9.

Furthermore, he saw off his regular season with 21 receptions for 247 receiving yards and three receiving touchdowns with 228 rushing yards.

Notably, the Patriots saw Patterson clinch two receptions for 14 yards with a 38-yard kick return and seven rushing yards as they overpowered the LA Rams 13-3 to win Super Bowl LIII with Julian Edelman.

Chicago Bears

Similarly, Patterson joined the Chicago Bears with a two-year, $10 million deal in March 2019.

Without delay, he made the Bears debut in their season opener against the Green Bay Packers, where they lost 10-3.

Notably, he had 294 kickoff return yards and four tackles in November only that earned him an NFC Special Teams Player of the Month.

To point out, he had the most kick return yards of 825 in 2019 and the second-highest yards per return of 29.5 in the whole NFL.

The kickoff return average of 29.9 comes behind Gale Sayers in Bears’ history, a Chicago Hall of Famer.

Atlanta Falcons

The wide receiver signed a one-year contract with the Falcons on April 15, 2021. He is currently playing with the team.
